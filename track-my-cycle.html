Severity Rating Scale

1 Not at all
The symptom is completely absent.
2 Minimal
Barely noticeable; does not affect daily life.
3 Mild
Present but easily managed.
4 Moderate
Clearly present; causes noticeable discomfort.
5 Severe
Intense; significantly impacts functioning.
6 Extreme
Overwhelming and incapacitating.

Rating Examples

Use these anchors to calibrate your responses
# Statement Examples — Level 1 (Not at all) → Level 6 (Extreme)
1 Depressed mood, hopelessness, worthlessness, or guilt Feeling perfectly content (1) → profound, paralyzing despair or intense guilt (6)
2 Anxiety, tension, or feeling keyed up / on edge Feeling completely relaxed (1) → constant, severe physical tension or panic (6)
3 Mood swings, sensitivity to rejection, or easily hurt feelings Emotional stability (1) → uncontrollable crying spells or extreme emotional sensitivity (6)
4 Anger or irritability Calm and patient (1) → explosive rage or constant, severe agitation (6)
5 Decreased interest in usual activities Full engagement in all interests (1) → total lack of interest or pleasure in any activity (6)
6 Difficulty concentrating Sharp and focused (1) → completely unable to focus on simple tasks or thoughts (6)
7 Lethargy, fatigue, or lack of energy Feeling energetic (1) → physically exhausted to the point that getting out of bed is impossible (6)
8 Increased appetite or specific food cravings Normal hunger patterns (1) → uncontrollable urge to binge or intense, constant cravings (6)
9 Sleep disturbance (hypersomnia or insomnia) High-quality, restorative sleep (1) → total insomnia or sleeping throughout the entire day (6)
10 Feeling overwhelmed or out of control Feeling in total control (1) → complete loss of control over one's life or emotions (6)
11 Physical symptoms (breast tenderness, bloating, headache, joint/muscle pain) No physical discomfort (1) → severe, debilitating pain or extreme bodily swelling (6)
